• What are your biggest concerns when it comes to space weather? 

Our inability to forecast when another such storm might hit, the extent of the global technology damage that it might cause, and the time for society to recover are my biggest concerns.

Severe space weather storms of the strength of the 1859 Carrington event are rare – by analyzing the molecules deposited on ice at the poles, we can look back to about 1600 and see evidence of space weather storms as well as their strength. During that span of nearly 400 years, only the Carrington event was of the magnitude that caused the global issues back in 1859. Smaller storms occur often, but the stronger ones typically occur in conjunction with the 11 year solar sunspot cycle. With only the one data point of the 1859 Carrington space weather storm, it is difficult if not impossible to forecast when another storm of that magnitude might hit. We can say with some confidence, however, that storms of about ¼ or less of that strength occur about once every 11 years on average.

  • Are we prepared for a Carrington-level event?

No, but some people and infrastructure companies are becoming more knowledgeable about these storms and are starting to take some steps to protect their systems against a complete shutdown. The big problem is that, unlike 1859, people worldwide are dependent on systems that use technologies that would be effectively turn off by a severe storm if a Carrington event hit today. Everything that we use relies on technology today – not just Internet and cell phones, but power, water, gasoline for cars, banking, traffic control, and even natural gas all use technologies that need power, and it is the electric power grid that would be hit the hardest by such a storm. Of course, satellites that provide communications, GPS navigation, weather monitoring, etc. would all be at high risk during such a storm. A recent study by the National Academy of Sciences estimated that it would take the entire world up to 10 years to recover from such an event. We would instantly become agrarian again. That is a frightening vision.

  • Why should folks on Earth pay attention to space weather?

The education and awareness of the general population to the magnitude of this threat is essential to enable legislation that would provide funds for upgrading our infrastructure defenses. There are some steps that individual homeowners can take to become more independent of the power grid and these infrastructure services so that if they are suddenly destroyed, these folks could take the lead for rebuilding. Education, research, and (to some extent) preparation would pay off if a Carrington event hit today.

Some of the preventative measures can only be implemented by service companies and the government, so the extent to which politicians are educated about this threat, the better protected we will be.

  • Do you expect that storms in the future will have an outsized effect on us because of our reliance on technology? Why or why not?

Yes. As I mentioned above, we are dependent on a wide range of services that are all interdependent, and the single point of failure is electric power. We rely more and more on technology every day.. if it all were suddenly taken away for 10 years, the impact would be devastating worldwide.

  • Is more being done about preparing for space weather now than in the past?

Yes, but not enough. Most of the service providers tend to be reactive, not proactive, especially if the required protection would be very costly. Our infrastructure is getting old and needs repair. Replacing it with a more robust system that would be immune to Carrington events would be very costly, much more that simply replacing what we have. This would require a major commitment, all based on an unknown future event. This is not an easy sale to make!

  • Anything else you'd like to add?

Yes. The words “weather storm” conjure up visions of hurricanes etc. Space weather storms are a much more severe form of the events that cause the Northern Lights. They are NOT like a snow storm or hurricane – there is no immediate physical presence that can be felt, other than the sky glowing in dancing colors brightly at night for days. These are caused by events on the sun which have something to do with clusters of sunspots occurring at the right place and the right time. These storms are not in any related to the Earth’s climate, and should not be thought of that way. They are their own special threat with origins on the sun, and they cannot be prevented. However, these storms are dangerous in that they can destroy our technology infrastructure worldwide. It would be almost immediate and would take years to recover.

Not a very cheery topic, but an important one! I hope this helps!
